M3 · Earnings Behaviour Map — ±15 bar window analysis
NANDI detects earnings events two ways: (1) from your uploaded calendar, (2) by detecting vol spikes where HV jumps >1.5σ from its 20-bar mean in a single bar (likely event). For each event, it maps the ±15 bar window: pre-event drift, event-day move, post-event continuation or reversal, and sector sympathy.
Upload earnings calendar (optional): Format: Symbol,Date (CSV) or {symbol:date} (JSON). If not uploaded, NANDI infers events from vol spikes.

M5 · Cross-Sector Return Correlation
When sector A fires bull signals, does sector B outperform in the next 5–10 bars? This maps lead-lag relationships in returns, not just signals. High positive correlation = sectors move together. High negative = natural diversification. Useful for: pre-positioning in correlated sectors, hedging with negatively correlated sectors.
